Walking in the world

Exploring the course of my dead self

Opening verse

I'd like to start by reading some verses from Ephesians 2.

1 And you hath he quickened, who were dead in trespasses and sins; 2 Wherein in time past ye walked according to the course of this world, according to the prince of the power of the air, the spirit that now worketh in the children of disobedience: 3 Among whom also we all had our conversation in times past in the lusts of our flesh, fulfilling the desires of the flesh and of the mind; and were by nature the children of wrath, even as others. 4 But God, who is rich in mercy, for his great love wherewith he loved us, 5 when we were dead in sins, hath quickened us together with Christ, (by grace ye are saved;) 6 And hath raised us up together, and made us sit together in heavenly places in Christ Jesus: 7 That in the ages to come he might shew the exceeding riches of his grace in his kindness toward us through Christ Jesus. 8 For by grace are ye saved through faith; and that not of yourselves: it is the gift of God: 9 Not of works, lest any man should boast.
— Ephesians 2:1-9 KJV

A conviction

The Spirit laid this conviction on me tonight, as I write this, that I am not thanking the Lord enough for what He did for me. I dug in the far corners of my memory for titles of songs I listened to in years past. I remembered a few (praise be to God, my memory is not the best) and looked them up on the internet. I was apalled by what I heard when I listened.

Beneath the surface

Once one digs beneath the surface of flashy beats and pleasing vocals, which are totally artificial, and analyses the lyrics of these songs, you will be appalled; appalled not only at the vices these people are indulging in, and the demons they have in them, but that, at least in my case, you, as a child, the one time you are as close to innocent as you will ever be in this world, listened to such things which are of the world.

Tarnished innocence

Whether younger Will understood the meaning of certain things in the media I "consoomed", I will never know, but it is beyond the question. By association with these things, my innocence was tarnished. I knew about how great smoking weed was, I knew about women and how prized having sex with them was, I knew about how cool drinking was; I could go on. Especially once I got to high school, I was surrounded by such degeneracy, such evil. One might say I walked according to the course of this world, according to the prince of the power of the air.

He held onto me

Truth be told, I don't know where I would have ended up if I had continued fulfilling the desires of the flesh and of the mind. I was never one of the "cool kids", but I did fit into a niche of bad people nonetheless. I did get invited to parties (okay, a party), I was around drink and drugs, I did things I "regret" (though it is worth noting I do not wish they didn't happen, and I thank God for allowing those situations to occur), and I believe it is entirely possible I would have gone to a far worse, far far darker place. And yet, even at the time, comparing myself to my peers, I didn't seem as troubled by addictions as them. I didn't smoke, in fact I found (and still find) it repulsive, I wasn't an alcoholic, but I was certainly no saint. At the time this only served to fuel my ego, my attitude that I was clearly superior to these people for not being as weak-willed as they were, but I can look back now and only say three words:

Thank you, Lord

Thank you, Lord. Thank you, Lord, for your protective hand over me. Thank you, Lord, for your allowing me to go through those trials. Thank you, Lord, for plucking me out of such desturctive, soul-destroying path, wide and accommodating of such vice, taking my hand and leading me to the narrow path of eternal life, leaving me dead to sin and alive in Christ, and filling me with your Spirit as an assurance of salvation. Thank you, Lord, for your Son, Jesus Christ my Saviour, the firstfruits of the resurrection, my guarantee of everlasting life with you, oh Lord, thank you.
